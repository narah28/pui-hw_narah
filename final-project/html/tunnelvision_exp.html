Peripheral sight, or side vision, is what you see on the outer edges of your visual field when looking straight ahead. Tunnel vision occurs when this peripheral sight is lost. If this happens, you will only be able to see something if you are directly looking at it. This type of vision loss is primarily caused by issues with the rods and cones — two types of photoreceptors, or light-sensing cells — in the retina. The retina helps you see by recognizing light and sending information to the brain.

Could be chronic or temporary

Treatment for tunnel vision typically follows the same process regardless of how advanced the condition. Doctors like to start with prescription medication. Next comes an option of laser therapy or else a combination of laser therapy and meds.The last treatment option is surgery.
